Biography

Sundar Pichai is an Indian-American business executive and the CEO of Alphabet Inc., the parent company of Google. He is known for his leadership and contributions to the technology industry, as well as his philanthropic work.

Early Life and Education

Sundar Pichai was born on July 12, 1972, in Chennai, India. His father was an electrical engineer, and his mother was a stenographer. Pichai was an excellent student and earned a scholarship to study metallurgical engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur. He later earned a master's degree in material sciences and engineering from Stanford University and an MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.

Career

Pichai started his career as a management consultant at McKinsey & Company before joining Google in 2004. At Google, he worked on several projects, including the development of Google Chrome, the company's web browser. He also oversaw the development of other popular products, such as Gmail and Google Drive. In 2015, Pichai was appointed CEO of Google, succeeding Larry Page. Under his leadership, Google has continued to innovate and expand its offerings, including the development of Google Assistant, Google Home, and Google Cloud. In 2019, Pichai was named CEO of Alphabet Inc., making him one of the most powerful people in the technology industry.

Philanthropy

In addition to his work in the technology industry, Pichai is also known for his philanthropic efforts. He and his wife, Anjali Pichai, have donated millions of dollars to various causes, including education and healthcare. In 2015, they donated $15 million to help fund a new initiative to fight Ebola in Africa. They have also donated to the Stanford School of Medicine and the Khan Academy.

Awards and Recognition

Pichai's contributions to the technology industry have not gone unnoticed. He has been named one of Time Magazine's 100 most influential people and was included in Forbes' list of the World's Most Powerful People. In 2020, he was awarded the Global Leadership Award by the U.S.-India Business Council.
